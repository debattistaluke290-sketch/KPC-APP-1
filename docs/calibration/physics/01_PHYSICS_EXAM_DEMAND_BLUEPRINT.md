# Physics Examination-Demand Blueprint (SEC 24, 2026)

Derived from the SEC 24 (2026) syllabus + 2021–2025 papers/reports. All demand is described **abstractly**; no official wording/values/scenarios reproduced.

## Recommended 10-topic structure (from 7 Foci / 8 LOs)

The syllabus has 7 Subject Foci but two are oversized for a 4-item diagnosis. The current app uses 9 topics; the **single evidence-based change** to reach 10 coherent topics is to **split the current "Waves, Light & Sound" into "Waves & Sound" and "Light & Optics"** — justified because LO1 explicitly demands *"ray diagrams to show the path taken by waves in media"* and optics (reflection, refraction, lenses) is a large, distinct assessable area. All other current topics map cleanly to LOs and are retained (one renamed for accuracy).

| # | Topic | Syllabus LO / Focus | Why a separate diagnosis |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Motion & Forces | LO2 (Motion, Forces & Energy) | kinematics + Newton's laws — core, high-frequency |
| 2 | Forces, Moments & Pressure | LO2 | equilibrium/moments, density, pressure — distinct skill set |
| 3 | Energy, Work & Power | LO2 | energy stores/transfer, power, efficiency |
| 4 | Thermal Physics | LO3 | states of matter, heat transfer, specific heat |
| 5 | Waves & Sound | LO1 | wave properties, v=fλ, sound |
| 6 | **Light & Optics** *(new split)* | LO1 | reflection, refraction, lenses, ray diagrams |
| 7 | Electricity & Circuits | LO4 | charge, current, Ohm's law, series/parallel, power |
| 8 | Magnetism & Electromagnetism | LO5 | magnets, fields, motor effect, transformers |
| 9 | Radioactivity | LO6 | atomic structure, radiation, half-life |
| 10 | Earth & Space | LO7 | Solar System, universe, scale |

Cross-cutting **LO8 (how physics works / practical / graphs)** is assessed *within* topics (v–t graph read-off in Topic 1, data interpretation elsewhere), not as a separate topic — matching how the exam embeds it.

**Limitation of 4 items/topic:** each topic samples only 3–4 subtopics of many; a topic label reflects *this sample*, not guaranteed mastery. Documented in the scoring model.

## Per-topic demand + 4-question plan

Legend: **F**=foundation, **S**=standard_exam, **A**=standard_exam(application), **U**=upper_exam. "Marks" = marks-equivalent.

### 1. Motion & Forces — LO2 (AC 2.1–2.3)
- Command words: state, calculate, describe. Misconceptions: confusing speed/velocity/acceleration; forgetting resultant force; a=Δv/t reading a single value.
- **F** (MCQ, 1): identify/define a quantity (e.g. what a velocity–time gradient represents). Recall.
- **S** (work, 2): acceleration from a v–t graph read-off (graph skill, LO8). Units m/s².
- **A** (work, 3): resultant force in a described everyday scenario → F=ma. Method selection.
- **U** (work, 3–4): braking with a speed-unit conversion (km/h→m/s) then F=ma. Multi-step.

### 2. Forces, Moments & Pressure — LO2
- Command words: calculate, explain. Misconceptions: wrong pivot distance; cm³↔m³; forgetting P=hρg vs P=F/A.
- **F** (work, 1): density = m/V, direct.
- **S** (work, 2): principle of moments, one unknown force.
- **A** (work, 2–3): liquid pressure P=hρg in a described context.
- **U** (work, 3): density with a cm→m unit conversion (volume in m³).

### 3. Energy, Work & Power — LO2
- Misconceptions: mixing GPE/KE; power vs energy; efficiency as % of wrong base.
- **F** (work, 1): GPE = mgh.
- **S** (work, 2): energy conservation GPE→KE, find speed.
- **A** (work, 2–3): power = work/time in a lifting scenario.
- **U** (work, 3–4): efficiency + power over time → input energy (kW→W).

### 4. Thermal Physics — LO3
- Misconceptions: conduction/convection/radiation needing a medium; Δθ vs final temp; good emitter = matt black.
- **F** (MCQ, 1): heat-transfer method (e.g. through a vacuum).
- **S** (work, 2): Q = mcΔθ, find energy.
- **A** (MCQ, 2): best emitter/absorber comparison (applied).
- **U** (work, 3): kettle/heater time from Q=mcΔθ and P.

### 5. Waves & Sound — LO1
- Misconceptions: amplitude vs wavelength; v=fλ rearrangement; MHz/kHz prefixes.
- **F** (work, 1): v = fλ direct.
- **S** (MCQ, 2): amplitude vs wavelength from a wave diagram.
- **A** (work, 2): sound wavelength from v and f in a context.
- **U** (work, 3): radio wavelength with a MHz→Hz prefix + standard form.

### 6. Light & Optics — LO1 *(new topic)*
- Subtopics: reflection (law), refraction (bending toward/away normal), critical angle/TIR (concept), lenses (converging, real/virtual image), ray diagrams. Command words: state, describe, calculate. Misconceptions: angles measured from surface not normal; refraction direction; image nature.
- **F** (MCQ, 1): law of reflection / angle measured from the normal.
- **S** (work, 2): refraction — refractive index n = sin i / sin r (values chosen to be clean).
- **A** (MCQ, 2): behaviour at a boundary / total internal reflection in a described case (applied concept).
- **U** (work, 3): lens or refractive-index multi-step (e.g. speed-of-light ratio, or magnification = image/object).

### 7. Electricity & Circuits — LO4
- Misconceptions: series vs parallel resistance; fuse rating; current the same in series.
- **F** (work, 1): Ohm's law I = V/R.
- **S** (work, 2): series resistance then current.
- **A** (MCQ, 2): choose correct fuse from P=IV (applied).
- **U** (work, 3): parallel branch currents summed.

### 8. Magnetism & Electromagnetism — LO5
- Misconceptions: like/unlike poles; step-up vs step-down; transformer current/voltage inverse.
- **F** (MCQ, 1): pole interaction (attract/repel).
- **S** (MCQ, 2): step-up vs step-down from turns ratio (concept).
- **A** (work, 2): transformer Vs from turns ratio.
- **U** (work, 3): ideal transformer power → primary current.

### 9. Radioactivity — LO6
- Misconceptions: alpha/beta/gamma penetration; isotopes; half-life counting.
- **F** (MCQ, 1): least penetrating radiation.
- **S** (work, 2): activity after n half-lives (clean multiple).
- **A** (MCQ, 2): isotope definition (applied to a described pair).
- **U** (work, 3): number of half-lives from activity ratio → time.

### 10. Earth & Space — LO7
- Misconceptions: light-year is time; ordering of astronomical scale; d = v×t with standard form.
- **F** (MCQ, 1): what a light-year measures.
- **S** (work, 2): distance = speed × time (Sun–Earth), standard form.
- **A** (MCQ, 2): order astronomical objects by size (applied).
- **U** (work, 3): distance light travels in a year, standard form multiplication.

## Final 40-question composition
10 topics × (1 F + 2 S[1 application] + 1 U) = **40**: 10 foundation, 20 standard_exam (10 application), 10 upper_exam. Type mix ≈ 27 `work` + 13 `mcq`, 0 free-text (Physics core is objectively markable — no AI marking). Estimated completion time ≈ 10 F×45 s + 20 S×90 s + 10 U×150 s ≈ **44 minutes** (diagnostic is not timed but this confirms exam-realistic demand).
