# Reference Validation Addendum

Companion to `00_REFERENCE_MATERIAL_INVENTORY.md`. Confirms the reference set actually used for calibration and its limitations. No files were re-renamed (the inventory is correct).

## Documents used

**Physics**
- `Physics_SEC_2026_Syllabus.pdf` — **primary content authority.** Read: Introduction, Subject Foci (7), Learning Outcomes (LO1–LO8), Programme Level Descriptors (Levels 1–3), Learning Outcomes & Assessment Criteria (sampled: Waves LO1; practical LO8), Scheme of Assessment (pp 43–44), Appendices 1–3 (maths notation, circuit symbols, SI units).
- Papers 2021–2025 (May+Sep) — difficulty/command-word/format evidence. **2025 (new "Level 1-2-3" format)** treated as the best structural match to the current syllabus.
- Marking schemes 2021 May, 2024 May — mark-allocation & accepted-answer conventions (old format).
- Examiners' reports 2021–2025 — common misconceptions & recurring weaknesses.

**Mathematics**
- `Mathematics_SEC_2026_Syllabus.pdf` — **primary content authority.** Read: Introduction, four strands, Subject Foci (10), Learning Outcomes (LO1–LO10), Level Descriptors, Assessment Criteria (sampled), Scheme of Assessment (pp 65–67), General Notes incl. **strand weightings**, Table of Formulae.
- Papers 2021–2025 (May+Sep), marking schemes 2023/2024/2025 May, examiners' reports 2021–2025.

## Key structural findings

| Item | Physics | Mathematics |
|---|---|---|
| Subject Foci | 7 | **10 (explicitly named)** |
| Learning Outcomes | 8 (LO1–LO8) | 10 (LO1–LO10) |
| Levels | 1 / 2 / 3 (L1 basic → L3 highest) | 1 / 2 / 3 |
| Exam (Paper II) | 100 marks, 2 h; Sec A 8–10 short (40), Sec B structured+graph (15), Sec C 3 long (45) | 100 marks, 2 h; 10–15 questions, answers on paper |
| Tiers | Levels 1–2 or 2–3 | Levels 1–2, 2–3, or 3–3* |
| Strand weighting (standard 2-3 tier) | not published as strand % | **Number 20% · Algebra 35% · Shape/Space/Measures 30% · Data 15%** |
| Calculator | (formula sheet given on paper) | Scientific (non-programmable) allowed; formula sheet given |

**Difficulty mapping used throughout:** syllabus **Level 1 → foundation**, **Level 2 → standard_exam**, **Level 3 → upper_exam**. This is a direct, defensible mapping to the rubric in `03_MATHS_PHYSICS_DIFFICULTY_RUBRIC.md`.

## Syllabus compatibility / format concern
- Both 2026 syllabi are the **current** authority (first teaching Sept 2022, first awards 2025). The **2025 papers already use the new Level 1-2-3 format** → best match. **2021–2024 papers (old format)** are used for difficulty/command-word evidence only, with caution.
- Calibration targets the **current syllabus content**, not any single paper's structure.

## Limitations created by missing evidence
1. **Physics: no new-format (2025) marking scheme.** Mark allocations for the current format are inferred from printed marks on the 2025 paper + 2024 scheme conventions + syllabus. Recorded as a limitation; not a blocker.
2. **Physics/Maths: marking schemes only for some years.** Sufficient to establish conventions; not exhaustive.
3. **No item-level statistics** (facility, discrimination) are available for any KPC question — all difficulty labels are **expert-judgement, not empirical**. This is the single biggest limitation and is why nothing here may be called empirically validated.

## Sufficiency verdict
- **Physics: sufficient to calibrate conservatively** (current syllabus + full paper run + examiner insight).
- **Mathematics: sufficient and well-supported** (current syllabus + strand weightings + three recent complete sets incl. new format).
- Neither subject can be **empirically** validated without a student pilot (facility/discrimination data). Expert calibration only.
