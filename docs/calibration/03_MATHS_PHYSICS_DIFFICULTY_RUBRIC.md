# Maths & Physics Difficulty Rubric

Difficulty categories used across both candidate banks, mapped to the SEC syllabus levels. **Level 1 → foundation, Level 2 → standard_exam, Level 3 → upper_exam.** `stretch` sits just beyond normal exam demand and is **excluded from the core 40** (optional only). Examples are described **abstractly** — no official wording, values or scenarios.

## Core-40 composition per topic (both subjects)
1 × `foundation` · 1 × `standard_exam` · 1 × `standard_exam` (application) · 1 × `upper_exam`.
Per subject total: **10 foundation, 20 standard_exam (10 of them application), 10 upper_exam.**

## Categories

### foundation (syllabus Level 1)
- **Reasoning steps:** 1 (single recall or one-step skill).
- **Cognitive demand:** recall / basic understanding.
- **Marks-equivalent:** 1–2.
- **Expected time:** 30–60 s.
- **Types:** MCQ, single-value `work`.
- **Fair:** familiar representation, all data given, one obvious method. **Unfair (avoid):** trick wording, obscure facts.
- *Physics abstract example:* state the SI unit of a named quantity, or one-step substitution into a given formula.
- *Maths abstract example:* convert a simple fraction to a decimal; find a percentage of a round amount.

### standard_exam (syllabus Level 2)
- **Reasoning steps:** 2 linked steps.
- **Cognitive demand:** understanding / routine application.
- **Marks-equivalent:** 2–3.
- **Expected time:** 60–120 s.
- **Types:** `work`, well-constructed MCQ, data read-off.
- **Fair:** ordinary exam demand, standard method, clear what is asked. **Unfair (avoid):** hidden multi-topic dependencies.
- *Physics:* find resultant force then acceleration; rearrange a formula once and substitute.
- *Maths:* solve a two-step linear equation; percentage increase/decrease.

### standard_exam — application variant (syllabus Level 2, applied)
- Same difficulty band as standard_exam but set in a **changed/real context** requiring the student to **select the relevant information and method** rather than plug into an obvious formula.
- **Reasoning steps:** 2–3; **cognitive demand:** application.
- *Physics:* a described everyday scenario where the student must choose which relationship applies.
- *Maths:* a worded money/ratio/measurement problem requiring method selection.

### upper_exam (syllabus Level 3)
- **Reasoning steps:** 3+ linked steps, or one step with a conceptual twist.
- **Cognitive demand:** application / analysis / synthesis, fully within syllabus.
- **Marks-equivalent:** 3–4.
- **Expected time:** 120–180 s.
- **Types:** multi-step `work`, higher-order MCQ, interpret-then-calculate.
- **Fair:** harder end of *normal* exam demand; unfamiliar but fair; all information present or derivable. **Unfair (avoid):** ambiguity, missing data, out-of-syllabus content, excessive arithmetic unrelated to the concept.
- *Physics:* a multi-stage energy/efficiency or transformer problem with a unit conversion.
- *Maths:* compound percentage change; combine Pythagoras with a further step.

### stretch (beyond normal exam demand — NOT in core 40)
- Non-routine synthesis, multiple representations, or a novel combination.
- Optional/separate; must **not** affect the estimated examination score.
- Drafted only if clearly labelled and stored outside the core bank.

## Fairness invariants (all categories)
- Fully solvable from the information given + syllabus knowledge.
- Exactly **one defensible marking outcome**.
- Difficulty comes from **genuine cognitive demand**, never from ambiguity, obscure trivia, reading load, or missing data.
- Every numeric answer has a justified accepted value + tolerance; every MCQ distractor reflects a real misconception.
